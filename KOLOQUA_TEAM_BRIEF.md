# Koloqua AI Language Lab — Team Brief

## Why we are doing this

Koloqua is more than a dialect. It is how millions of Liberians talk, joke, argue, pray, teach, and do business every day. Right now, the world's best voice assistants, translation apps, and AI tools do not understand Koloqua. That means our language is slowly being left out of the digital economy.

**Koloqua AI Language Lab exists to fix that.** We are building the first community-owned dataset of Koloqua speech and text, reviewed by Liberians, for Liberians. Our goal is simple: make Koloqua a first-class language in AI.

## What the platform does

The pilot is a web app where team members can:

- **Record** prepared Koloqua sentences directly in the browser.
- **Transcribe** Koloqua audio and pair it with Standard English meaning.
- **Review** other people's submissions for quality, accuracy, and naturalness.
- **Manage campaigns** for themed collection drives (e.g., market scenes, school lessons, health conversations).
- **Track contributions and earnings** through a transparent rewards ledger.
- **Export clean datasets** for researchers, educators, and future AI training.

Everyone gets a role — contributor, reviewer, or admin — and only sees what they are permitted to see.

## Why you should join

1. **Own a piece of the future.** The spellings, pronunciations, and usage standards we define now will shape how Koloqua is represented in AI for years.
2. **Build real skills.** You will learn how voice data, natural language processing, quality review, and data governance work.
3. **Protect the culture.** Language is memory. If we do not digitize Koloqua on our own terms, someone else will do it badly later.
4. **Earn from your knowledge.** Your ear for Koloqua is valuable. We want to pay you for that expertise, not extract it for free.
5. **Work with a purpose.** This is a national pilot. Schools, linguists, tech partners, and funders are watching what we build together.

## How contributors get paid

We believe contributors deserve real rewards for high-quality work. Here is how the payment model works in the pilot:

- **Validated contributions earn rewards.** When you record or transcribe a sentence and an independent reviewer approves it, your contribution is marked valid and added to your rewards ledger.
- **Reviewers earn too.** Reviewers who catch errors, suggest corrections, and maintain quality standards also receive rewards for approved review actions.
- **Quality matters.** Submissions flagged for poor audio, wrong transcription, or synthetic voice will not earn. This keeps payouts fair and the dataset clean.
- **Transparent ledger.** Every approved action is logged. You can see your balance, pending validation count, and payout history inside the app.
- **Payout requests.** Once your validated balance reaches the minimum threshold, you can request a payout. During the pilot, payouts are processed manually after a quick identity and phone-money verification.
- **Future scale.** As funding and partnerships grow, we will automate payouts directly to mobile money or bank accounts, add tiered bonuses for top contributors, and run themed campaigns with extra rewards.

**Important:** Payments in this first phase depend on funding, identity checks, and mobile-money agreements. We are not promising overnight riches. We are promising a fair, transparent system where good work is tracked and rewarded.

## What we need from you

- Record a few sentences when a campaign opens.
- Transcribe carefully. Write what you actually hear, not what you think should be there.
- Review honestly. Approve good work, flag bad work, and explain corrections.
- Keep your login credentials private. The demo password is shared inside the team only.
- Tell one other person. The more native speakers we have, the better the dataset becomes.

## The bigger picture

This is step one. After the pilot we will add:

- A proper backend with encrypted audio storage.
- Invitation-based authentication.
- Guardian consent flows for contributors under 18.
- Duplicate, silence, and synthetic-voice detection.
- Dataset versioning and public releases under a community-approved license.
- Whisper fine-tuning only after the dataset is clean and evaluation splits are frozen.

## Our promise

- Informed, revocable consent for every contributor.
- Minimal personal data. Identity and language data stay separate.
- Two independent reviews before any recording is used for training.
- No voice cloning or commercial use without separate, explicit consent.
- Clear governance. The community helps decide standards, not just the core team.
